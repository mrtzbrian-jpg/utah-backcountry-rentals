Where to backpack from Utah County

Classic Utah backcountry routes

High Uintas Wilderness

Naturalist & Amethyst Basin

Utah's premier alpine backpacking — granite basins, dozens of lakes and long summer days off the Mirror Lake Highway.

Uinta Mountains

Kings Peak

At 13,528 ft, Utah's highest summit is a bucket-list multi-day trek through Henrys Fork Basin.

American Fork Canyon

Mount Timpanogos

Overnight to Emerald Lake below Utah County's iconic 11,752 ft peak via the Timpooneke or Aspen Grove trail.

Wasatch Front

Lone Peak Wilderness

Rugged granite cirque straddling Utah and Salt Lake counties — a demanding, rewarding overnighter.

Nebo Wilderness

Mount Nebo

The highest peak in the Wasatch Range (11,933 ft), south of Payson along the Nebo Loop.

Big Cottonwood Canyon

Lake Blanche

A steep, scenic classic to a glacial lake beneath Sundial Peak — a short drive north in Salt Lake.

Backpacking rental FAQs

Where can I rent backpacking gear in Utah?+

Take a Hike Rentals rents backpacking gear with local pickup in Saratoga Springs, Utah — an easy drive from Provo, Lehi and Salt Lake City and close to the Uinta and Wasatch trailheads. Reserve online and pick up on your trip dates.

What backpacking gear can I rent?+

Multi-day backpacks, lightweight tents, sleeping bags, water filters, bear canisters and satellite communicators — or a complete solo, couples or family bundle.

Is renting backpacking gear worth it?+

Yes — a quality backpacking kit costs $1,000+ to buy. Renting lets you get out for a weekend for a fraction of that, and try gear before committing to your own.

Do you rent bear canisters and water filters?+

We do. Both are available individually and are included in most of our backpacking bundles.
